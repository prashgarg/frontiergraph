# Paper Methods Lock Note

## Main lock

The paper should now treat the empirical object in this order:

1. **Measurement anchor:** a missing directed link between endpoints
2. **Surfaced research object:** a path-rich or mechanism-rich frontier question
3. **Routed overlays:** context-transfer and evidence-type expansion when the internal semantic layer gives strong support
4. **Internal support layer:** ontology-vNext, family-aware comparison, and reviewed edge typing

That is the stable ordering.

## What goes in the core paper

The main paper should foreground:

- the missing directed link as the clean prospective object
- the graph-based retrieval and reranking pipeline
- the path/mechanism rendering as the main human-facing object
- the benchmark comparison against a preferential-attachment-style baseline
- the interpretation as a screening tool under attention constraints

## What moves later in the draft

These are now extensions or internal support layers:

- context-transfer routing
- evidence-type-expansion routing
- family-aware comparison
- ontology-vNext concept typing
- policy-semantics and relation-semantics overlays

They matter, but they should not be introduced before the reader understands the basic benchmark and surfaced object.

## Plain-language formulation

The paper should say, in effect:

- we start from a missing directed link because it is a clean and prospectively testable measurement anchor
- but researchers do not inspect bare missing links
- they inspect richer question objects, usually about plausible pathways or mechanisms
- our graph-based system helps screen those richer questions under realistic attention constraints

## What not to do

Do not let the main paper read as if:

- ontology-vNext is the main contribution
- routed object families are the main benchmark object
- the paper is a theory of full research prediction

That would make the draft harder to parse and less credible.

## Current internal freeze rule

Ontology-vNext should now be treated as **frozen except for future reviewed override tables**.

That means:

- no new family promotion for now
- no broader heuristic growth for the ontology layer
- no ranking changes tied to ontology-vNext in the next paper pass
