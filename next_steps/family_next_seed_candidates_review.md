# Family Next-Seed Candidates Review

## Scope

This note reviews the two remaining broad candidate themes after the typed audit and the split trade-family promotion:

- `environment climate concepts`
- `labor income demand concepts`

The goal is to decide what should become the **next narrow seed candidates**, not whether the broad themes should be promoted as reviewed families in their current form.

## Decision

Do **not** promote either broad theme as-is.

Instead, treat them as sources for narrower future seed families.

## Why the broad themes should not be promoted directly

### `environment climate concepts`

Current members:

- `energy consumption`
- `energy intensity`
- `environmental regulation`
- `green innovation`
- `renewable energy`
- `renewable energy consumption`

This theme is meaningful, but too broad as an active reviewed family.

It mixes:

- energy-use objects
- energy-transition objects
- policy/intervention objects
- and innovation outcomes

That is the same failure mode we already learned to avoid with the old trade/urban family.

### `labor income demand concepts`

Current members:

- `income distribution`
- `income inequality`
- `willingness to pay (WTP)`

This theme is not coherent enough as one reviewed family.

The problem is `willingness to pay (WTP)`.
It behaves much more like a valuation / preference object than a distributional outcome.

So this should not be promoted as one family.

## Narrower next-pass candidates

### Candidate A: `energy transition and efficiency concepts`

Proposed members:

- `energy consumption`
- `energy intensity`
- `renewable energy`
- `renewable energy consumption`

Why this is the best narrow slice from the environment/climate queue:

- the concepts are closer in kind
- the grouping is more coherent than the full broad theme
- it avoids mixing policy and innovation concepts into one umbrella family

### Candidate B: `distributional outcome concepts`

Proposed members:

- `income distribution`
- `income inequality`

Why this is the best narrow slice from the labor/income queue:

- the concepts are genuinely related
- they are clearly not synonyms
- they are much more coherent together than either is with `willingness to pay`

### Hold out for now

Do not include these in the next reviewed seeds yet:

- `environmental regulation`
- `green innovation`
- `willingness to pay (WTP)`

These may belong in future families, but not in the narrow seeds above.

## Important practical finding

At the current shortlist stage, these narrow next-pass candidates still produce **zero within-family pairs** in the active enriched shortlist.

So they are not yet good candidates for immediate promotion into the active reviewed family layer.

That matters.

It means the right status for them is:

- **reviewed next-pass candidates**

not:

- **active reviewed families**

## Recommendation

Keep the active reviewed family set unchanged for now.

Carry these forward as the next calibrated seed candidates:

1. `energy transition and efficiency concepts`
2. `distributional outcome concepts`

If later shortlist or routed outputs begin surfacing within-family links for these slices, they become strong promotion candidates.
