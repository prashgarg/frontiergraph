# Paper Curated Examples

## Main example set

Use these as the current paper-facing example set.

### 1. Baseline surfaced object

- Pair: `price changes -> CO2 emissions`
- Surface form: `What nearby pathways could connect price changes to CO2 emissions?`
- Why keep it:
  it is readable, path-rich, and still looks like a plausible substantive frontier question without needing the routed overlay to become interpretable

### 2. Context-transfer overlay

- Pair: `financial development -> green innovation`
- Surface form: `Where should we test the financial development -> green innovation relation next?`
- Why keep it:
  this is the clearest example of the routed layer changing the question from a generic pathway prompt into a setting-transfer prompt

### 3. Evidence-type-expansion overlay

- Pair: `willingness to pay -> CO2 emissions`
- Surface form: `What evidence should come next for the willingness to pay -> CO2 emissions relation?`
- Why keep it:
  it is still the clearest example of the evidence overlay, even though it should not dominate the headline shortlist because it sits in a crowded CO2-centered region

### 4. Family-aware comparison

- Pair: `state of the business cycle -> house prices`
- Surface form: `Within the macro cycle and price concepts family, is the state of the business cycle -> house prices link more specific than sibling links involving price changes and rate of growth?`
- Why keep it:
  it is the strongest compact example of family-aware comparison working as an internal extension object

## Examples to suppress from the headline set

Do not use these as headline examples in the next draft:

- `income tax rate -> CO2 emissions`
- `regional heterogeneity -> CO2 emissions`
- `income tax rate -> environmental quality`
- `environmental regulation -> sustainable development`

These rows are not necessarily bad.
They are just too exposed to redundancy, crowding, or semantic overlap to carry the paper’s examples.
