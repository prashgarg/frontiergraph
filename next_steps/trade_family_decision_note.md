# Trade Family Decision Note

## Candidate under review

- `trade urban structure concepts`

Members:

- `exports`
- `imports`
- `industrial structure`
- `industrial structure upgrading`
- `trade openness`
- `urbanization`

## Decision

Do **not** promote this as one reviewed family.

## Why

The candidate is not random, but it is too broad and internally mixed to treat as one reviewed family.

The internal relation pattern is uneven:

- `industrial structure upgrading <-> industrial structure` is very strong
- `exports <-> imports` is clearly coherent
- `urbanization <-> trade openness` is plausible but weaker
- the cross-links between the trade objects and the structural-transformation objects are much weaker

So the candidate is not one clean "related but not identical" family.
It is really mixing at least two subfamilies.

## Better split

### 1. Trade flow / openness family

- `exports`
- `imports`
- `trade openness`

This is the stronger trade-focused core.

### 2. Structural transformation / urban structure family

- `industrial structure`
- `industrial structure upgrading`
- `urbanization`

This is the stronger transformation / structure core.

## Why splitting is better

It preserves the real signal without forcing unlike concepts into one family.

That matters because the whole point of the family layer is:

- related but not identical

not:

- everything that co-appears in the same broad empirical neighborhood

## Promotion decision

### Do not promote now

Do not promote the broad `trade urban structure concepts` family as a reviewed seed.

### What to do instead

Keep the broad family only as a candidate artifact.

If we do a next reviewed family-seeding pass, it should test:

- `trade flow and openness concepts`
- `structural transformation and urbanization concepts`

as separate candidate reviewed seeds.

## Bottom line

This candidate is valuable because it surfaced a real thematic neighborhood.

But it should be treated as:

- one broad discovery artifact

not:

- one active reviewed family

The correct next move is to split it, not to promote it wholesale.
