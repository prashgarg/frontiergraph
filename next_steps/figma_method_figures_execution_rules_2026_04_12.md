## Figma Method Figure Execution Rules

These rules apply to the paper method figures in Figma.

### Objective

Build paper-quality method figures that are compact, information-dense, and visually legible in LaTeX.

### Design rules

1. One figure answers one question only.
2. Use one worked example that reads like a real economics question.
3. Do not use generic workflow art.
4. Assume titles, captions, and long figure notes live outside the figure.
5. Avoid wasted space. Boxes should hug their contents.
6. Use stylized but serious visual design, not placeholder diagram aesthetics.
7. Reuse the same visual language across figures once the first figure is locked.

### Process rules

1. Work one figure at a time.
2. After every substantial change, render and inspect the figure visually.
3. Do not stop after writing to Figma. Review the screenshot and fix obvious problems first.
4. If something is visibly broken, fix it before showing the figure.
5. Do not move to the next figure until the current one is defensible in a paper draft.

### Quality bar

A figure is not ready if any of the following remain:

- arrowheads or labels are visibly misaligned
- the excerpt reads awkwardly because of layout rather than wording
- there is obvious dead space that does not help legibility
- nodes or boxes are oversized relative to their text
- the figure is technically correct but visually weak

### Decision rule

If the figure is technically correct but visually weak, keep iterating.
