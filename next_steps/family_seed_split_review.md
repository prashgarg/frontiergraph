# Family Seed Split Review

## Scope

This note reviews the promotion of two narrower reviewed families:

- `trade flow and openness concepts`
- `structural transformation and urbanization concepts`

These replace the earlier idea of one broad mixed trade/urban candidate family.

## Result

The split looks correct.

It improved the active reviewed family layer without recreating the earlier mixed-family problem.

## Current reviewed-family state

The rebuilt prototype summary now shows:

- `reviewed_family_count = 6`
- `family_seed_rows = 25`
- `family_candidate_rows = 9`

So the active seed layer grew, while the unresolved candidate queue got smaller and more focused.

## Why the split was worth doing

### 1. The trade-flow family is coherent

Reviewed members:

- `exports`
- `imports`
- `trade openness`

This family now yields plausible family-aware objects such as:

- `Within the trade flow and openness concepts family, is the imports -> exports link more specific than sibling links involving trade openness?`

That reads like a real comparative object rather than a forced grouping.

### 2. The structural-transformation family is coherent

Reviewed members:

- `urbanization`
- `industrial structure`
- `industrial structure upgrading`

This family now yields plausible family-aware objects such as:

- `Within the structural transformation and urbanization concepts family, is the urbanization -> industrial structure link more specific than sibling links involving industrial structure upgrading?`

Again, this is much more coherent than the earlier broad trade/urban bundle.

### 3. The broad trade/urban family is no longer the right abstraction

That earlier candidate was mixing:

- trade-flow concepts
- openness concepts
- structural transformation concepts
- urbanization concepts

Those are related in many papers, but not in one clean family relation.

The split respects the “should relate, but should not merge” rule much better.

## Candidate queue implication

The remaining candidate queue is now smaller and more useful.

Top remaining themes are now things like:

- `environment climate concepts`
- `labor income demand concepts`

That is better than repeatedly resurfacing a family we already knew was too broad.

## Decision

Keep both split families as active reviewed seeds.

Do not revive the broad trade/urban candidate.

If we revisit this region later, it should be through:

- narrower reviewed families
- or explicit `related` links across families

not by collapsing them back into one umbrella family.
