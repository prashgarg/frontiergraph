A concise methods note

How FrontierGraph turns the literature into a ranked agenda.

FrontierGraph is built around a narrow principle: AI may help extract graph structure from text, but the ranking itself should remain deterministic, auditable, and interpretable by domain experts.

Stance

What the tool is trying to do

  • Surface promising missing directed links in the economics literature.
  • Make those suggestions inspectable rather than opaque.
  • Support researcher judgment instead of pretending to automate scientific strategy.

Constraint

What AI is allowed to do

  • Read papers and extract candidate graph edges.
  • Stop there.
  • The ranking, filtering, and evidence presentation stay deterministic after graph construction.

Pipeline

Four stages

1

Extract

Convert papers into a claim graph of directed concept-to-concept relationships.

2

Generate candidates

Enumerate missing links implied by underexplored pairs, open paths, and incomplete motifs.

3

Score

Combine graph signals with penalties for highly connected hubs and boosts for low-contact corridors.

4

Inspect

Let users drill into mediators, paths, papers, and exportable idea briefs for each candidate.

Signals

What drives the ranking

  • Underexplored concept-pair gaps.
  • Path-implied missing direct edges.
  • Motif completion in open triads.
  • Field distance, prior co-occurrence, and hub penalties.

Evaluation

How it is checked

  • Retrospective backtests over multiple time horizons.
  • Score decomposition and evidence inspection in the app.
  • Separate workstreams for expert validation and prospective challenge design in the repo.

Current beta limits

What this public version is not doing yet

The live beta is read-only and economics-focused. It does not yet model downstream scientific value, support saved agendas, or offer a custom domain for the app itself. The current stack is intentionally lightweight so the product can be tested before the infrastructure becomes more complex.

  • Economics graph only in the current public beta.
  • Read-only SQLite backend mounted into Cloud Run.
  • No user accounts or collaboration layer yet.
  • Method and outputs remain versioned in the GitHub repo.
